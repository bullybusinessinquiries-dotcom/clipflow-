Overview

ClipFlow is built with privacy as a foundational principle. This Privacy Policy explains exactly how data is handled when you use ClipFlow.

The core principle: Your content stays on your machine. We do not collect, store, or transmit your videos, audio, transcripts, or personal data to ClipFlow servers.

What We Do Not Collect

ClipFlow explicitly does NOT collect, store, or have access to:

  • Your video files or audio content
  • Your video transcripts (they stay on your machine)
  • Your clip metadata or video editing configurations
  • Your API credentials or access tokens
  • Usage patterns, analytics, or telemetry data
  • Engagement metrics or performance data about your content
  • Information about your audience or viewers
  • Personal information beyond what you voluntarily provide

No Tracking: We do not use cookies, pixels, analytics tracking, or any other technology to monitor your use of the Tool. ClipFlow does not know how often you run the automation, what keywords you use, or how many clips you create.

Local Processing & Transcription

Whisper Transcription (100% Local):

All video transcription is performed entirely on your local machine using OpenAI's open-source Whisper model. This means:

  • Your video audio NEVER leaves your computer
  • Transcripts are generated locally and stored on your machine only
  • No cloud transcription service has access to your content
  • Processing happens offline (after initial model download)
  • You maintain 100% privacy over transcript content

Processing Details: When you run ClipFlow, it:

  • Downloads the Whisper model to your machine (one-time, ~74MB for "base" model)
  • Processes your video locally using that model
  • Generates transcript files and stores them in your local directory
  • Never connects to external transcription APIs or services

API Credentials & Security

Credential Storage: Your API credentials for TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and Twitter/X are stored in a local `.env` file on your machine. They are:

  • Stored locally on your computer only
  • Never transmitted to ClipFlow servers
  • Never logged or printed in debug output
  • Protected by your machine's file permissions
  • Your sole responsibility to keep secure

How Credentials Are Used: When ClipFlow runs, credentials are used to:

  • Authenticate directly with official platform APIs
  • Download your TikTok livestreams (TikTok API)
  • Upload clips to connected platforms (official APIs)
  • Retrieve platform metadata (video titles, descriptions, etc.)

All API communication uses official OAuth 2.0 authentication and goes directly between your machine and each platform. ClipFlow does not act as a proxy or intermediary.

Platform API Communications

When ClipFlow uploads clips to your social media accounts, it communicates directly with official platform APIs. These communications are governed by each platform's own privacy policy:

Platform Data Shared Privacy Policy
TikTok Video files, captions, hashtags TikTok Privacy Policy
Meta (Instagram) Video files, captions, hashtags Meta Privacy Policy
YouTube Video files, titles, descriptions Google Privacy Policy
Twitter/X Video files, text, links Twitter/X Privacy Policy

Important: ClipFlow does not have access to data about how platforms use the clips you upload. Each platform has its own data collection practices, which are outside ClipFlow's control.

Log Files & Monitoring

ClipFlow generates local log files each time it runs. These logs contain:

  • Timestamp of execution
  • Number of videos downloaded
  • Number of clips created
  • Upload status for each platform
  • Errors encountered (if any)
  • Total execution time

Log Storage: All logs are stored locally on your machine in the `~/content-automation/logs/` directory. They are:

  • Stored only on your computer
  • Not transmitted to ClipFlow or any external service
  • In your control — you can delete them anytime
  • Formatted as JSON for easy parsing and monitoring

Email & Notifications

If you configure email notifications, ClipFlow can send you:

  • Weekly summary reports (clips created, upload status)
  • Error alerts (if something fails during automation)

Email Handling:

  • Your email address is stored in your local `.env` file only
  • ClipFlow does not store your email on any server
  • Emails are sent directly from your configured SMTP provider (your ISP, Gmail, etc.)
  • ClipFlow does not log or retain email addresses
  • You can disable email notifications by removing the email from your `.env` file

Data Retention

Raw Video Files: Automatically deleted from your machine after clips are created. This saves disk space while preserving your transcripts and clips for reference.

Transcript Files: Retained locally for your reference, analysis, and as a record of transcribed content. You control whether to keep or delete these.

Clip Files: Retained locally after being uploaded. You can delete them to save space, or keep them as a backup.

Configuration Files: Your `.env` and keyword configuration files are stored locally and are your responsibility to keep or delete.

Log Files: Retained indefinitely in your logs directory. You can delete them anytime.

Security Measures

What ClipFlow Does:

  • Uses official OAuth 2.0 authentication for all platform APIs
  • Requires explicit credential storage in your local environment file
  • Never prints credentials or tokens to logs or output
  • Never transmits your data to external servers
  • Uses HTTPS for all API communications

What You Should Do:

  • Keep your `.env` file secure and never commit it to public repositories
  • Use strong, unique API credentials from each platform
  • Rotate API credentials periodically (recommended annually)
  • Do not share your configuration files or credentials with others
  • Monitor your platform accounts for unauthorized activity

Contact & Privacy Rights

Since ClipFlow does not collect personal data and processes everything locally on your machine, traditional data subject rights (GDPR, CCPA, etc.) have limited applicability. However:

Your Control: You have complete control over all data on your machine. You can:

  • Delete all configuration files, logs, and clips anytime
  • Stop using ClipFlow at any time with no data retention
  • Opt-out of email notifications by removing your email from `.env`
  • Disconnect from any platform API by removing credentials

Privacy Questions: For questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy, please contact us through:

  • Your TikTok profile (if registered with us)
  • GitHub repository issues or discussions

Policy Updates: We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Continued use of ClipFlow after updates constitutes acceptance of the new policy. We recommend reviewing this policy periodically.
